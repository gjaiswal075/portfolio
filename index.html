Our Founder's Journey

"I started at 20, not in a boardroom, but with a thermal power plant deal.

At just 20, I purchased a 35MW thermal power plant and sold it to a major chemical company. That single move taught me more about business, risk, and negotiation than any classroom could.

Soon after, I founded G Jaiswal Ventures Pvt Ltd and entered commodity trading dealing in iron, copper, and aluminium. One of my boldest decisions came when I acquired 600MW thermal power plant spares, valued at over $20 Million. This inventory continues to be monetized strategically through industrial buyers.

My vision expanded globally when I became a Director and Equity Partner at ARDAU Community Waste Power Limited, a UK-based clean energy company. Together with ARDAU, I'm now also developing a 150MW solar power plant in India, a major leap into renewable energy.

Simultaneously, I'm leading a nickel ore processing venture starting with imports from Indonesia, and planning to localize extraction within India once domestic reserves open up."

Portfolio

Strategic Assets Across Power, Commodities, and Clean Energy

Over the years, We have invested in, acquired, and developed high value ventures across India and the UK spanning energy, commodities, spares, and industrial infrastructure.

These are 600 MW equipment pairs stored within the same OEM containers, comprising critical components such as slurry pumps, LP and HP bypass valves, Voith couplings, globe valves, gate valves, coal monitoring systems, electrical actuators, and more. Primarily imported machinery, these units are essential for the operation and maintenance of thermal power plants. Sourced from leading manufacturers across the globe including China, the UK, the USA, the Czech Republic, Germany, and other countries they reflect a diverse and high-quality international supply chain.

We have imported Laterite nickel ore from Indonesia specifically for research and development purposes. From this ore, we aim to produce valuable materials such as nickel, cobalt, and nickel pig iron. The processing will follow two distinct routes: the HPAL (High Pressure Acid Leach) method will be used to extract nickel and cobalt, while the conventional smelting process will be employed for producing nickel pig iron. This initiative marks a significant step towards advancing our in-house metallurgical capabilities.

This is the 35 x 2 MW power plant that was originally operational in Korba, Chhattisgarh. After the plant went into liquidation, we acquired it through the liquidation process and successfully relocated it to Punjab. There, we revived and recommissioned the plant with the intent of putting valuable infrastructure back to work for the country. This marked the beginning of our journey in the power generation sector and laid the foundation for future projects.
